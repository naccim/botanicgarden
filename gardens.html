Jardin à la française

Located west, the French garden is characterized by its regular routes, its architectural symmetry and water mirrors . The current architecture of this garden was adopted in 1913, following a competition open to landscape architects , two of which were selected MM . Regnier and Guion .

It extends over an area of 7 hectares , with a length of 600 m intercepted by five successive terraces ; allowing a view of the sea, a large platform of more than half a hectare , supported against the bottom by a long colonnaded gallery , this garden ends at the rue Mohamed Belouizdad

Jardin à l'anglaise

The eastern part of the garden, the garden comprises English-style housing various collections of trees and palm trees of tropical and subtropical origin, thus restoring a semblance of Nature .



The English garden is composed of winding alleys and plots with irregular contours ; we find a large pool including Japanese carp and aquatic plants of different origins.re , supported against the bottom by a long colonnaded gallery , this garden ends at the rue Mohamed Belouizdad
